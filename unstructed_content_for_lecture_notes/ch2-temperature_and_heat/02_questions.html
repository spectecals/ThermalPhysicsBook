Questions

2.9. Questions#

  1. The world’s power consumption is currently around 20 TW, and growing! (1 TW = 1012W.) Burning one ton of crude oil (which is nearly seven barrels worth) produces about 42 GJ (1 GJ= 109J). If the world’s total power needs were to come entirely from burning oil (a large fraction currently does), how much oil would we be burning per second?

  2. The molar heat capacity of gold is 25.4 Jmol−1K−1. Its density is 19.3×103 kgm−3. Calculate the specific heat capacity of gold and the heat capacity per unit volume. What is the heat capacity of 4×106 kg of gold? (This is roughly the holdings of Fort Knox.)

  3. Explain the differences and similarities between the definitions of the kelvin and Celsius temperature scales. (5)

  4. The triple point of CO2 is at 5.11 atm and 216.6 K. Describe the phase changes that occur in a sample of CO2 when:

    • The temperature increases from 200 K to 273 K at 1 atm.

    • The temperature increases from 200 K to 273 K at 100 atm.

    • The pressure is reduces from 100 atm to 1 atm at 273 K.

    Include sketches to show these phase changes relative to the phase boundaries. (5)

  5. What is the minimum quantity of ice at -10C you need to bring 500 g of liquid water at 60C to 0C? (5)
